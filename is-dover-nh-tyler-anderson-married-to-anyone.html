Tyler Anderson Arrest And Investigation Revealed

Tyler Anderson, a 30-year-old resident of Dover, NH, has been arrested for allegedly sending threatening text messages directed at Republican presidential candidate Vivek Ramaswamy and attendees of a campaign event scheduled for Monday.

The arrest was made under the charge of transmitting threats in interstate commerce. According to court documents, Anderson’s menacing messages were sent in response to a campaign notification about a political event in Portsmouth.

His disturbing texts explicitly threatened violence, stating, “Great, another opportunity for me to blow [the candidate’s] brains out!” and “I’m going to kill everyone who attends and then [expletive] their corpses.”

The targeted event was Ramaswamy’s Town Hall at the Roundabout Diner in Portsmouth, where a buffet-style breakfast was scheduled for 8:30 a.m.

The Ramaswamy campaign confirmed that they were indeed the target of Anderson’s threats and expressed gratitude to law enforcement for their swift response.

Likewise, Federal agents acted promptly, obtaining a search warrant for Anderson’s residence on Central Avenue in Dover.

During the search, they seized a Samsung Galaxy S10E phone believed to have sent the threatening messages, along with firearms. Anderson admitted during an FBI interview to sending similar threats to “multiple other campaigns.”

Further investigation revealed a disturbing pattern of behavior, as agents discovered additional threatening text messages in a deleted folder on Anderson’s phone.

These messages, sent to a different candidate last Wednesday, included similar menacing language, indicating a potentially broader threat.

The Ramaswamy campaign released a statement thanking law enforcement for their professionalism in handling the matter and expressing concern for the safety of all Americans.

Anderson now faces a serious charge that could lead to up to five years in prison, three years of probation, and a maximum fine of $250,000.
